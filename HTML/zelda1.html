A Masterclass in open world design

Many of us have grown up with "Zelda" and "Super Mario" games. They are the two biggest franchises of Nintendo and for some reason even today they manage to keep our interest undiminished. The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild is of course a slightly different title than the rest, but it manages and maintains the same magic that Ocarina of Time had back then.

Obviously, the first thing to mention about Breath of the Wild is its new open world. The tremendous freedom and sense of adventure that we feel, from the very first minutes, is the greatest achievement of the game. Facing the world of Hyrule for the first time you will see a magical landscape in front of you, while you will notice certain places on the map that you will immediately want to explore, such as temples, cities, enemy camps and ruins.

It's important to understand that Nintendo didn't fall into the trap of creating landscapes that could exist on Earth. Everything seems to be neatly in place. That's why you'll see huge mountains and valleys, and even quite a few crumbling ruins. Almost every type of possible climate and environment will have a representation and it is properly placed to provide us with a wide variety. Also, the world is filled with dozens of things to do on your way, which will often disorient you, almost to the point where you forget where you were supposed to go and what you were supposed to do.

Cooking and ingredients

The truth is that our survival through some crafting system is not an unusual phenomenon in adventure titles, but it is usually done in a very rough way. In Breath of the Wild we rely on the materials we collect from the environment, such as fruit and meat from animals, to cook or eat directly. The system is relatively very simple and does not need much explanation from NPCs, we just take the ingredients and throw them into a boiling cauldron (there is usually one in villages), and if the combination of ingredients is right, then we will have a delicious food. When we create a new food, we automatically "learn" the recipe so we can know how we created it. Of course, if you want you can consume some of the materials as well, but they will heal you very few hearts and many times it is not worth wasting them like that.

The foods we cook can help us on our journey in ways other than Heal. For example, if you add some hot peppers to your recipe, it will also give you a buff for a few minutes which helps you withstand the cold. Which is quite useful in snowy areas where you literally die... of the cold. In general, you will notice that all the recipes are neat and to some extent they are also quite realistic, that is, the result they will have on us will make sense.

Battle System

The combat system of Breath of the Wild is quite simple, but it also has a degree of difficulty as the game progresses. In the beginning, you should collect all the items from the opponents, because as you fight, your items will break and even with just 4-5 simple hits. Of course, when the object goes to break your next hit will always be critical, so you might even be able to kill your opponent. But in addition to the basic hits, you will be able to use shields that will also break with use, and if you make a successful parry you will have the opportunity to counter hit with an impressive slow motion. Don't hesitate to pick up some pieces from your opponents that break during the battle to finish them off with, like for example their... hands!

Of course, we can also throw our weapons to kill an enemy when he escapes, but the long range combat doesn't stop there. Archery also plays a very important role in the game. With the press of a button Link whips out his bow and if possible can take out large opponents without even getting close to him. The game even has a variety of arrows that can either create an explosion or burn someone alive. As in several other games, here too if we hit someone in the head we will do a "headshot" that will cause a lot of damage, while sometimes killing instantly. Of course, all this happens the first time we play, because as we progress the enemies become more and more powerful.

But all this happens mainly to the monsters we meet outside on the street. When we meet a boss things are quite different. Bosses usually have a strategy that we must follow to beat them and it is not enough to simply hit them many times. In addition, we should avoid almost all the hits, because especially in the last bosses they are deadly, unless we have enough hearts that then maybe we can withstand 2-3 hits.

Pros

  • Awesome graphics and gorgeous environment.
  • Vast world.
  • Good use of the open world element.
  • Very nice story and satisfying battles.
  • Game duration.

Cons

  • A few minor issues in the technical area.
  • A little strange handling.

Verdict

"The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild" could be described as a "Masterpiece" for fans of the series and not only. It provides us with endless material to explore, even after its completion, which is provided relatively early for those in a hurry. A few minor issues encountered by the title (which have been fixed through various updates) are the only reason this game can't reach the maximum rating.

9.7/10
